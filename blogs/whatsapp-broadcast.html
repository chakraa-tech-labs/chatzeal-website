What is WhatsApp Broadcast?
WhatsApp Broadcast is a feature that allows you to send a message to several persons simultaneously as a private message through Whatsapp. In contrast to group chats, in which all participants are able to read other participant's replies, a broadcast message only reaches the individual recipient as a private chat.
Broadcast is very suitable for dissemination of announcements, news, or promotional material to a targeted audience including friends, family, customers, or clients. In order that they are to be effective it is necessary for the receiver to have your number saved in the receiver's contacts so that the broadcast message will reach them.
Key Features of WhatsApp Broadcast:
  • One-to-Many Messaging: Send one message to multiple contacts at once.
  • Privacy Maintained: All recipients are treated as individual private chats, not as a group message.
  • Reusable Lists: Broadcast lists can be compiled and saved to allow periodic communication.
  • Customizable Content: Broadcasts support text, images, videos, documents, and more.

How to Create, Edit, and Delete Broadcast Lists
1. For Regular WhatsApp Users:
Create a Broadcast List
  1. Open WhatsApp.
  2. On Android:
    • Tap the three-dot menu and select New Broadcast.
  3. On iPhone:
    • Go to the Chats tab, tap Broadcast Lists, and select New List.
  4. Add contacts to the list (only those who’ve saved your number will receive the message).
  5. Tap Create or the checkmark icon to save your list.
Edit a Broadcast List
  1. Open the broadcast list you want to edit.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (Android) or Edit List (iPhone).
  3. Add or remove contacts as needed.
  4. Save the changes.
Delete a Broadcast List
  1. Open WhatsApp and navigate to the broadcast list.
  2. Long press the list name (on Android) or swipe left on the list (on iPhone).
  3. Select Delete to remove the list.
2. For WhatsApp Business App Users:
The WhatsApp Business app works similarly to the regular WhatsApp app but includes tools like labels to organize your contacts for better segmentation.
Create a Broadcast List
  1. Open WhatsApp Business.
  2. On Android:
    • Tap the three-dot menu and select New Broadcast.
  3. On iPhone:
    • Go to the Chats tab, tap Broadcast Lists, and select New List.
  4. Add contacts (e.g., labeled groups like “VIP Customers” or “Pending Orders”).
  5. Tap Create or the checkmark icon to save the list.
Edit a Broadcast List
  1. Open the broadcast list you want to modify.
  2. On Android:
    • Tap the three-dot menu > Broadcast List Info.
    • Edit the list by adding or removing contacts.
  3. On iPhone:
    • Tap the Edit List button.
    • Make your changes and save.
Delete a Broadcast List
  1. Locate the broadcast list in your chats or Broadcast Lists section.
  2. Long press (Android) or swipe left (iPhone) on the list.
  3. Select Delete to remove the list.
3. For WhatsApp Business API Users
The WhatsApp Business API is designed for large-scale messaging and integrates with CRM platforms or business dashboards for managing broadcast lists.
Create a Broadcast List
  1. Set Up the API:
    • Integrate the API with your CRM or dashboard through a WhatsApp Business Solution Provider.
  2. Create a Segment:
    • Use your CRM to filter and segment your audience based on preferences, location, or interactions.
  3. Compose the Message:
    • Draft a message using WhatsApp-approved templates for broadcast campaigns.
  4. Send the Broadcast:
    • Use the API’s dashboard to send your message to the targeted audience.
Edit a Broadcast List
  1. Open your CRM or messaging dashboard connected to the API.
  2. Update the recipient list by modifying the filters or manually adding/removing contacts.
  3. Save the changes to ensure the updated list is ready for your next broadcast.
Delete a Broadcast List
  1. Navigate to the broadcast campaign in your CRM or API dashboard.
  2. Remove the entire audience segment or delete the campaign itself.
  3. Confirm the deletion if prompted.

Pro Tips for Making the Most of WhatsApp Broadcast
Effectively using WhatsApp Broadcast goes beyond simply sending messages—you need to fine-tune your communication to make it engaging, impactful, and well-received by your audience.
1.Segment Your Audience
  • Why it Matters: Broadcasting to your contact list may not always be productive unless you segment your audience into categories such as "VIP Customers," "New Clients," or "Friends and Family”.
  • How to Do It: Label your contacts on WhatsApp Business to effectively classify them into meaningful groups.
2.Keep Messages Short and Engaging
  • Why it Matters: Shorter, crisper messages are more readable and also more likely to receive a response.
  • How to Do It: Use formatting like bold and italics to highlight important information and keep your message visually appealing.
3.Personalize Whenever Possible
  • Why it Matters: Tailored messages feel more authentic and establish trust between senders and recipients.
  • How to Do It: To include the recipient name, mention their recent purchases, or customize the message to their liking.
4.Use Multimedia for Better Engagement
  • Why it Matters: Messages with images, video or GIFs are more interesting than plain text.
  • How to Do It: Share pictures of your products, promotional videos, or pamphlet files (for example, brochures or menus) via your broadcasts.
5.Respect Privacy and Get Opt-Ins
  • Why it Matters: Recipients have to remember your number in order to get broadcast messages, and spam can hurt your image.
  • How to Do It: Proactively nudge your audience to save your number with an easily understood opt-in phone message (such as: “Want to be in the loop about our latest offers? Save our number and reply with YES to join!”)
6.Choose the Right Timing
  • Why it Matters: Sending messages at the wrong time (e.g., late at night) can lead to being ignored—or worse, blocked.
  • How to Do It: Schedule broadcasts at the time of greatest engagement, for example, during the morning, lunch break, and early evening.
7.Monitor Responses and Engage Quickly
  • Why it Matters: Broadcasters frequently provoke responses, and responsive dialogue plays an important role in relationship building.
  • How to Do It: Set aside time to respond to messages promptly and address any inquiries.
8.Reuse Broadcast Lists
  • Why it Matters: Now that you have a list, you don't have to regenerate it for later broadcasts.
  • How to Do It: Applying the same list to send follow-ups, announcements or periodic updates to your segmented audience.
9.Avoid Overloading Your Audience
  • Why it Matters: Sending too many messages can annoy recipients and lead to being blocked.
  • How to Do It: Spread out your broadcasts and concentrate on providing useful content and not just promotions.
10.Experiment, Analyze, and Improve
  • Why it Matters: Not every broadcast will be a hit. Learning what works best for your audience is crucial.
  • How to Do It: Track delivery and read receipts to measure engagement. Experiment with different message formats, timings, and content types.Use feedback to refine your future broadcasts.
Avoid These Broadcast Blunders
Avoid these common mistakes to make your WhatsApp Broadcasts more effective and engaging:
1.Recipients Haven’t Saved Your Number
  • Messages won’t be delivered unless your number is saved.
  • Solution: Ask recipients to save your number
2.Spamming with Too Many Messages
  • Frequent messages can annoy people.
  • Solution: Send messages sparingly and only when necessary.
3.Sending Generic Messages
  • Impersonal messages feel spammy.
  • Solution: Personalize messages using names or tailored content.
4.Ignoring Replies
  • Not responding to recipients' replies damages trust.
  • Solution: Engage promptly when someone replies
5.Not Segmenting Your Audience
  • Sending irrelevant messages can backfire.
  • Solution: Organize contacts into categories for targeted messaging.
6.Sending Messages at Odd Hours
  • Poor timing can lead to messages being ignored.
  • Solution: Send messages during normal or peak engagement times.
7.Cluttered or Long Messages
  • Overloading information makes messages hard to read.
  • Solution: Keep messages short and to the point.
8.Overusing Media
  • Too many images or videos can feel unprofessional.
  • Solution: Only use media that adds value.
9.Not Tracking Results
  • You won’t know what’s working.
  • Solution: Monitor read receipts and responses to improve.
10.Violating WhatsApp Policies
  • Sending unsolicited messages can get you banned.
  • Solution: Always get consent and follow WhatsApp’s guidelines.
FAQs About WhatsApp Broadcast
Below are some of the most frequent questions regarding WhatsApp Broadcasting and their answers.
1.What's the maximum number of contacts I can put on a broadcast list?
The number of contacts you can sum into a broadcast list is 256. However, in the WhatsApp Business API, this limit may be significantly higher depending on configuration.
2.Do the recipients have to save my number to get a broadcast?
Yes, in order to get broadcast messages recipients have to store your number in their contacts.
3.Can recipients reply to a broadcast message?
Yes, but their reply will appear as a private chat with you. Other recipients in the broadcast won’t see the reply.
4.Can I reuse a broadcast list?
Yes, the broadcast list once created is stored and can be repeated to send out future messages.
5.Can I send multimedia through a broadcast?
Yes, images, videos, audio, documents, and links, can be sent by broadcast.
6.What happens if someone deletes my number?
If the receiver deletes your number, it is no longer possible to receive his/her broadcast message.
7.Can I see who has read my broadcast message?
Yes, the delivery and read receipts are displayed per recipient separately in the broadcast message info.
8.Is there a cost for using WhatsApp Broadcast?
No, using WhatsApp Broadcast is free as you need internet connection. For WhatsApp Business API, costs may apply.
9.Can I schedule a broadcast?
The WhatsApp app doesn’t support scheduling directly, but third-party tools (for Business API) may allow scheduling.

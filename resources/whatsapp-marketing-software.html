Business Challenges in Selecting a WhatsApp Marketing Tool
If a business decides to buy WhatsApp for marketing and customer interaction, they do not randomly choose any application. They thoughtfully confirm whether the tool meets their requirements, saves time, and enhances their customer service. Here’s what they usually look for:
1. Should we use the WhatsApp Business App or API? (App vs. API Confusion)
A WhatsApp solution should align with business needs and scale accordingly. Businesses ask:
  • Is it easy to use for small businesses? (WhatsApp Business App – Simple but limited features)
  • Can multiple users access the same account? (Limited to five linked devices on the Business App)
  • Does it offer automation and scalability? (WhatsApp API – Ideal for all businesses, but requires third-party integration)
  • Can it integrate with CRMs and eCommerce platforms? (WhatsApp API allows advanced integrations for seamless workflows)
  • Which option is best for growth-focused businesses? (API provides automation, bulk messaging, and multi-user access for scalability)
2.What Can the Whatsapp Marketing Tool Do? (Features & Capabilities)
A useful WhatsApp marketing tool should help businesses communicate in a simple and speedy way. Businesses ask:
  • Is it possible to send messages in bulk to hundreds of customers? (Bulk Messaging)
  • Is it possible to configure automatic answers to typical questions? (Chatbots & Auto-Replies)
  • Can my team work together to handle customer messages? (Shared Team Inbox)
  • Can I schedule messages for later? (Scheduled Campaigns)
  • Is it possible to monitor the effectiveness of my messages? (Analytics & Reports)
If the tool does not provide these features, companies may go for another solution.
3. Will WhatsApp Block My Messages? (WhatsApp API Compliance)
In WhatsApp, there are strict regulations to stop businesses from spamming customer
  • Is this tool officially approved by WhatsApp? (WhatsApp API Access)
  • Will my messages be delivered properly? (High Deliverability Rate)
  • Does the tool help me follow WhatsApp’s rules? (Avoiding Bans)
Using a tool that violates WhatsApp’s policies can get a business blocked or banned from WhatsApp!
4. Is It Worth the Price? (Cost & Budget Considerations)
In WhatsApp, there are strict regulations to stop businesses from spamming customer
  • How much does it cost per month? (Subscription Plans)
  • Do I have to pay per message sent? (WhatsApp API Charges)
  • Does the product offer a free trial so that I can test it beforehand? (Trial or Demo Availability)
Businesses need a solution that fits within their budget and has no surprises.
5. Is It Easy to Use? (User Experience & Setup)
Nobody appreciates complicated software, so companies search for a tool that is:
  • Easy to set up. They don’t want to spend weeks learning how to use it.
  • Easy to use - The dashboard should be intuitive and easy to use.
  • No coding needed - Businesses prefer tools where they don’t have to hire a developer.
  • Quick response to customer interactions – When a customer responds to a bulk message, the tool should provide a provision to trigger an instant call to action (e.g., redirecting them to a landing page, booking form, or chatbot flow).
  • Smart chat routing & assignment – Once a customer engages, their query should be automatically assigned to the right team member or department, ensuring faster resolution and better customer experience.
The tool should ensure smooth customer interaction handling and team collaboration without manual effort.
6. Will It Work with My Existing Business Tools? (Integrations)
More businesses are already using other application to handle customers, such as
  • CRM systems like Zoho, HubSpot (for the management of customer data).
  • E-commerce sites such as Shopify (to manage orders sales).
  • Payment gateways for in-chat payments.
Business check if the WhatsApp tool can connect to these systems so they don’t have to manually update customer information.
7. Can I Get Help If Something Goes Wrong? (Customer Support)
In order to help businesses during times of need, it is necessary to have a tool that delivers and manages support as needed by the business. They ask:
  • Is there a customer support team? (Live Chat, Email, or Phone Support)
  • Will I get help setting it up? (Onboarding & Training)
  • What if I need technical help later? (Troubleshooting Assistance)
If a business has problems with the tool and there’s no support team to help, they may look for another option.

Different businesses use WhatsApp in different ways. They also verify whether the tool can be adapted to their requirements.
For an Online Store (E-commerce):

Businesses want a tool that fits their industry and helps them achieve their goals.

Best Practices for WhatsApp Marketing
WhatsApp isn’t just another messaging app—it’s a powerful marketing tool that lets businesses connect with customers instantly. But to truly make an impact (and avoid common pitfalls), you need to follow best practices to keep your audience engaged, compliant, and eager to interact.
Let’s break it down into five key strategies to help you make the most of WhatsApp marketing.
1. Make It Personal
Use names, customize content, and keep it human!
Nobody likes generic messages like “Hello Customer.” It feels robotic and impersonal. Instead, use personalization to grab attention and build relationships.
Example:
Don’t: “Your order is confirmed.”
Do: “Hi Sarah! Your order #12345 is on its way. Get ready for something awesome!”
How to personalize effectively:
  1. Address customers by name
  2. Segment your audience based on interests & past behavior
  3. Send targeted messages (“Hey Alex, your favorite shoes are back in stock!”)
Tip:
Integrate WhatsApp with CRM tools like HubSpot or Zoho to automate personalization without losing the human touch.
2. Stay on WhatsApp’s Good Side
Follow WhatsApp Business Policies to avoid bans
WhatsApp has strict rules to protect users from spam. If you don’t play by the rules, you risk getting blocked.
Key compliance tips:
  1. Get customer consent before messaging them
  2. Use pre-approved message templates for promotional messages
  3. Respect opt-outs—if someone replies “STOP”, remove them immediately
  4. Avoid spamming people with too many messages too quickly
Tip:
Always use WhatsApp Business API instead of personal numbers to stay compliant and scale your campaigns safely.

Send messages when people are most likely to respond

4. Balance Chatbots & Human Support
Automate common queries, but keep agents for complex issues
Chatbots can handle FAQs fast, but human agents add the personal touch when needed.
Best way to balance both:
  1. Let chatbots answer simple questions like store hours & order tracking
  2. Route complex issues (like refunds or VIP customers) to human agents
  3. Use AI chatbots to analyze customer sentiment & escalate urgent cases

Customer: “Can I change my delivery address?” →Chatbot transfers to a live agent
Tip:
Use smart routing so priority customers always get quick human support.

Measure your campaign performance to grow smarter
If you don’t measure your results, you won’t know what’s working (or what’s failing!).
Key WhatsApp marketing metrics:
  1. Open Rate – How many people opened your messages?
  2. Response Rate – Are they replying?
  3. Click-Through Rate (CTR) – Did they tap on your links?
  4. Conversion Rate – Are messages leading to sales?
  5. Opt-out Rate – Are people unsubscribing?
Tip:
Use A/B testing (send different message styles & see what works best). Over time, you’ll refine your approach for higher engagement & more sales.
